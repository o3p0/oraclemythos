The World Tree Yggdrasil

The tree that connects all nine realms of Norse mythology.

The World Tree Yggdrasil

Chapter 1: The Roots of Existence

Yggdrasil’s roots stretch into Asgard, Jotunheim, and Niflheim, each connecting to a sacred well. In Asgard, the Well of Urd is guarded by the Norns, who weave the fates of gods and men. In Jotunheim, Mimir’s Well grants unparalleled wisdom, while Niflheim’s spring of Hvergelmir sustains the tree amidst Nidhogg’s gnawing.

Chapter 2: The Realms and the Balance

Yggdrasil connects all nine realms of Norse cosmology, symbolizing the interconnectedness of life, death, and rebirth. Beneath its branches, the gods hold council, mortals thrive, and the eternal struggle of chaos and order plays out.

Chapter 3: Decay and Renewal

Though majestic, Yggdrasil is not immune to decay. Nidhogg gnaws at its roots, while the stags of the realms graze on its branches. Yet, its resilience mirrors the Norse belief in the cyclical nature of destruction and renewal.
