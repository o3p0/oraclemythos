Egyptian Mythology

Egyptian Mythology Overview

In the shadow of the pyramids and along the fertile banks of the Nile, Egyptian mythology unfolds—a realm where gods govern both life and death, and where kings are seen as divine. The Egyptians believed the universe was sustained through Ma’at, the principle of cosmic order, which the gods and pharaohs worked to maintain.

Egyptian mythology speaks of eternal cycles, from the daily rebirth of the sun to the soul’s journey through the afterlife.
"With a pantheon that rules over the sky, earth, and underworld, Egyptian mythology shaped the heart of one of history’s greatest civilizations."

Notable Myths

The Creation of the World

In the beginning, there was only the primordial waters of chaos, from which the god Atum arose. He created the first gods, Shu (air) and Tefnut (moisture), who then gave birth to Geb (earth) and Nut (sky).

The Death and Resurrection of Osiris

Osiris, a just king, was murdered by his jealous brother Set. Isis gathered the pieces of Osiris and resurrected him, making him the ruler of the underworld.
