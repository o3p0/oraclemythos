Chinese Mythology

Chinese Mythology Overview

Chinese mythology contains stories about the creation of the world, the gods, goddesses, and the human realm. Gods like the Jade Emperor and figures like the dragon are central to the mythos, representing cosmic power and harmony.

Chinese mythology is filled with tales of divine rulers, celestial beings, and forces of nature working in harmony to maintain cosmic balance.
"In the heavens and on earth, the balance of yin and yang creates all things, from the smallest of creatures to the vastness of the universe."

Notable Myths

The Creation of the Universe by Pangu

In the beginning, the universe was an egg of chaos. Pangu emerged, separating the heavens and the earth. As he held them apart, his body became the elements of the world after his death.

Nuwa Creates Humanity

Nuwa molded humans from clay, giving them life and creating the human race. After a great flood, she also repaired the sky, saving the world from chaos.

The Jade Emperor Governs the Cosmos

As the ruler of the celestial court, the Jade Emperor governs the universe, balancing the forces of yin and yang and maintaining order among the gods and mortals.
