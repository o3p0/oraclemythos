Norse Mythology

At the edge of the Nine Realms, where gods walk with mortals and giants loom in the shadows, the threads of fate are woven, and all beings prepare for the twilight of the gods—Ragnarok.

Norse mythology is filled with sagas of bravery, betrayal, and destiny, reflecting the Vikings' view of a harsh and unpredictable world.
"In the realm of thunderous skies and ancient oaths, the gods are bound to meet their doom—yet still, they fight."

Notable Myths

Creation of the World

From the void, a great frost giant named Ymir arose. When he was slain by Odin and his brothers, his body became the world—the earth from his flesh, the mountains from his bones, and the oceans from his blood.

Ragnarok

The end of the world is foretold in Norse myth. A great battle between gods and giants will shatter the realms.

Fenrir's Prophecy

Fenrir, the monstrous wolf, is prophesied to kill Odin during Ragnarok. The gods bound him with a magical ribbon, knowing that one day, he will break free and devour their king.
