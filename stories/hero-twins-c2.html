Setup – The Summons to Xibalba

The Lords of Death Call the Twins

Time passed, and Hunahpu and Xbalanque grew into clever, fearless tricksters. They spent their days playing ball, their laughter echoing through the land.

But the sound reached beyond the mortal world.

Deep within Xibalba, One Death and Seven Death heard the echoes of the past—the game they thought they had ended.

"They sound like their fathers," One Death growled.

"They will share their fathers’ fate," Seven Death hissed.

Messengers of Xibalba climbed from the underworld, carrying an invitation wrapped in false honor.

"The Lords of Xibalba request your presence in their court," they declared.

The twins exchanged a glance. They knew this was no mere invitation—this was a trap.

Yet they did not hesitate.

"We accept."

For they were not their fathers.

They would play the game.

But they would not be the ones to lose.

Conflict – The Journey & Deadly Trials

The Road to the Underworld

The path to Xibalba was long and treacherous. Rivers of blood and pus blocked the way, their waters writhing with unseen horrors.

The twins, however, stepped across them as if they were mere puddles—unshaken, untouched.

At last, they arrived before the throne of the Lords of Death.

"Welcome," One Death sneered.

"We have prepared trials for you," Seven Death said, grinning.

The trials of Xibalba had broken the first Hero Twins.

But Hunahpu and Xbalanque were no ordinary challengers.

Trial 1 – The Dark House

The twins were sent into a house of endless blackness, meant to disorient and terrify them.

They laughed.

Instead of fumbling in the darkness, they simply stayed still until dawn, outwitting the trial without effort.

Trial 2 – The Razor House

A room filled with blades that moved on their own, sharp as obsidian.

The twins spoke to the blades as if they were living things.

"Be still," they whispered.

And so the blades did not move.

Trial 3 – The Jaguar House

The next house was filled with jaguars, their golden eyes glowing in the dark.

Instead of running, the twins tossed bones into the air, distracting the beasts until sunrise.

Trial 4 – The Fire House

Flames rose around them, consuming everything.

But the twins remained untouched, for they had learned to dance within the flames.

Trial 5 – The Bat House (Camazotz’s Domain)

This house was different.

It was the dwelling of Camazotz, the bat god—a creature of death, a shadow of the night itself.

The twins lay still, hidden within their blowguns, waiting for morning.

But in the darkness, Camazotz stirred.

Without warning, the bat swooped down.

And with a single stroke of his claws—he took Hunahpu’s head.

Resolution – The Defeat of Camazotz & The Twin’s Triumph

A Trick Against Death Itself

For the first time, Xbalanque stood alone.

His brother’s headless body lay beside him, silent.

The Lords of Xibalba laughed in triumph.

"They have lost," they said.

But Xbalanque only smiled.

In the morning, the bats carried Hunahpu’s head to the court of Xibalba, placing it upon the ballcourt as the new game ball.

The Lords cheered. "Let us play," they declared.

But they had already lost.

As the game began, Xbalanque called to the animals of the land, gathering the creatures of the earth.

With their help, he crafted a new head for Hunahpu, binding his soul back into flesh.

And as the game reached its peak—Hunahpu stood up, whole once more.

The Lords of Xibalba froze in terror.

The twins had done what no mortal had ever done before.

They had defied death itself.

And the game was not over.

Not yet.
