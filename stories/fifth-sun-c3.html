The Last Offering

The Gods Must Die

The gods stood before Tezcatlipoca, the Smoking Mirror, the great and shadowed one.

He, who knew the secrets of life and death, spoke first.

"The sun demands blood," he said. "We must give ourselves."

A silence fell over the heavens.

These were the gods of creation‚Äîthe architects of the world. Without them, who would rule? Who would shape destiny?

But there was no other way.

The gods nodded.

One by one, they stepped forward.

Quetzalcoatl, the Feathered Serpent, the bringer of wisdom, placed his hand over his heart.
Tlaloc, the Rain Giver, the bringer of storms, bowed his head.
Chalchiuhtlicue, the Lady of Water, the mother of rivers and lakes, raised her arms to the sky.

And Tezcatlipoca, the smoke and shadow, smiled one last time.

Then, with one swift motion, they cut open their chests, letting their blood spill upon the earth.

The heavens shook.
The wind howled.
The ground drank the blood of the gods.

And at last, the sun began to move.

For as long as the sun moved, its fire must be fed.

And so, the world of the Fifth Sun was born.
