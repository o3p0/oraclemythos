Setup – The Gods Shape the First Humans

The Birth of Creation

Before the world had voices, before humans walked the earth, the gods gathered in the sky.

The Heart of Sky (Huracan), the great storm god, stood with Sovereign Plumed Serpent, the wise creator.

"The earth is made," said Sovereign Plumed Serpent. "The mountains rise, the rivers flow, and the sky stretches far."

"Yet it is empty," murmured Heart of Sky, his thunderous voice echoing through the heavens. "We are not praised. Who will remember our names?"

The gods turned to the earth below. They would craft beings to speak, to honor, to worship them.

And so, they reached down and took the mud of the earth into their hands.

They shaped the first humans, molding their bodies, smoothing their limbs.

They breathed life into them.

For a moment, all was still.

The first humans opened their eyes.

They rose, trembling, their limbs weak. Their skin was soft, damp, and fragile, crumbling at the slightest touch.

And when they tried to speak, their voices were hollow, empty whispers, like the wind passing through reeds.

The gods frowned.

"They cannot stand strong," said Heart of Sky. "They melt away with the rain."

"They do not hold wisdom," said Sovereign Plumed Serpent. "They cannot praise us."

These were not the true people.

Conflict – A Flawed Creation

The Mud People’s Fate

The mud people walked, but their steps were uncertain.

They spoke, but their words were meaningless echoes, unable to form prayers or gratitude.

They crumbled, dissolving back into the earth.

"This is not the way," the gods decided. "They are weak, unfinished."

The gods had wanted worshippers—beings who could honor them, who would offer maize and sing their names to the sky.

But these creatures could not even hold themselves together.

They were blind to the world, deaf to the gods.

And so, with a single breath, the gods washed them away.

The first humans were no more.

Resolution – The Search for a New Creation

The Gods’ Work is Not Done

The earth remained silent.

No voices called to the heavens.

No hands built temples.

No hearts beat with the fire of devotion.

The gods stood above the world, watching.

"This was only the first attempt," said Sovereign Plumed Serpent. "We will try again."

"We will make them stronger," said Heart of Sky. "We will give them form that will last."

But what material was worthy of creation?

The gods turned to the trees, their roots deep in the soil, their bodies unyielding.

"Perhaps wood will give them strength."

And so, the gods reached for the trees of the world, preparing for their next creation.

But strength without soul would bring a new disaster.
