A World Without Light

The Fourth Sun had died.

The world lay in shadow, the heavens silent. The wind carried only whispers of destruction, the remnants of the last age. Nothing moved. Nothing grew.

The gods stood in Teotihuacan, the sacred place where the fate of the cosmos was decided.

"If there is no sun," said the gods, "there is no world."

But to create a new sun, a great sacrifice would be required.

The gods turned to one another.

"Who among us will give their life?"

There was silence.

Then, a voice rose.

"Yes," they said. "A god of power and beauty is fit for the heavens."

And so, they prepared the Trial of Fire.

A great pyre was built, its flames stretching toward the heavens.

"Let the gods prove their courage," they declared. "Let them leap into the fire."

Nanahuatzin and Tecciztecatl stood before the flames. The test had begun.
